About Me

I am a faculty member with a Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering at the University of Vermont. My work focuses on forward-thinking R&D engineering with a strong background in dynamics, robotics, augmented reality, cyber-physical systems, and control systems.

I have successfully designed and prototyped complex systems to solve engineering challenges, resulting in 9 peer-reviewed scientific publications, a patent, and numerous certificates from top world universities. My research expertise spans robotics, augmented reality, structural health monitoring, cyber-physical systems, wind turbines, control, dynamics, and vibrations.

As a self-motivated individual with an adaptable attitude, I bring strong project management and analytical skills to meet tight deadlines in fast-paced environments. I am passionate about improving the quality of life through technology, particularly through the development of novel robotic and wireless sensors in augmented reality overlayed networks.

Research Interests

My research encompasses several interconnected areas that contribute to our understanding of robotics, augmented reality, and structural health monitoring. I am particularly interested in exploring cyber-physical systems, wind turbine control, and intelligent mechanisms for space applications.

Robotics & Augmented Reality

Development of mobile robots and sensor nodes in overlayed augmented reality wireless networks, with specific focus on infrastructure health monitoring. Successful implementation in collaboration with three universities on a $4 million NSF project.

Morphing Antennas & Space Systems

Designing and dynamic modeling of novel morphing antennas using intelligent mechanisms for space deployments in NASA projects. Focus on innovative solutions for aerospace applications.

Wind Turbine Control & Vibration

Designing and evaluating semi-active control systems for vibration suppression of semi-submersible offshore wind turbines with tuned liquid multi-column dampers.

Teaching

Teaching Philosophy

My teaching philosophy centers on fostering critical thinking and hands-on learning in engineering education. I believe in connecting theoretical concepts to real-world applications, particularly in robotics, control systems, and structural monitoring. I engage students through interactive problem-solving sessions, laboratory experiments, and project-based learning that mirrors current research challenges. My goal is to inspire students to become innovative engineers who can tackle complex problems with both analytical rigor and creative thinking.

Courses

ME 3220: Dynamic Systems and Controls

Level: Undergraduate

Introduction to dynamic systems modeling, analysis, and control design. Covers Laplace transforms, transfer functions, and feedback control systems with applications to mechanical and robotic systems.

ME 4950: Senior Design in Robotics

Level: Undergraduate

Capstone design course focusing on robotic system development. Students work in teams to design, build, and test autonomous robots, integrating mechanical design, sensors, and control algorithms.

ME 8000: Advanced Robotics and Automation

Level: Graduate

Advanced topics in robotics including motion planning, computer vision, machine learning applications, and human-robot interaction. Emphasis on current research trends and practical implementation.

Publications

Below is a selection of my recent publications with abstracts and related materials. For a complete list, please visit my Google Scholar profile.

Journal Publications

2024

Indoor Infrastructure Maintenance Framework Using Networked Sensors, Robots, and Augmented Reality Human Interface

A. Fath, et al.

Future Internet, MDPI, May 15, 2024

Abstract: This paper describes the use of networked smart technologies embedded with machine learning (ML) and presented in electronic formats to better inform homeowners and occupants about safety and maintenance issues. The demonstrated technologies include robotic sensing in confined areas, LiDAR scans of structural shape and deformation, moisture and gas sensing, water leak detection, network embedded ML, and augmented reality interfaces with multi-user teaming capabilities.

Article BibTeX
2024

MARSBot: A Bristle-Bot Microrobot with Augmented Reality Steering Control for Wireless Structural Health Monitoring

A. Fath, et al.

Micromachines, MDPI, January 29, 2024

Abstract: We describe the fabrication of a microrobot with bristle-bot locomotion using a novel centrifugal yaw-steering control scheme. The microrobot operates in a network consisting of an augmented reality headset and an access point to monitor infrastructures using augmented reality (AR) haptic controllers for human–robot collaboration.

Article BibTeX
2017

Surface effect on the biaxial buckling and free vibration of FGM nanoplate embedded in visco-Pasternak standard linear solid-type of foundation

A. Fath, et al.

Meccanica, Springer, April 1, 2017

Abstract: Nonlocal elasticity theory in conjunction with Gurtin–Murdoch elasticity theory is employed to investigate biaxial buckling and free vibration behavior of nanoplate made of functionally graded material (FGM) and resting on a visco-Pasternak standard linear solid-type of the foundation.
